Definitions

1. An equation is a statement that has two expressions that are equal. Example: x−3=1, 5y+3=3y+11

2. In an equation, the unknown is referred to as the variable. Example: In the equation 4x −18 = 2 − x , x is the variable.

3. When we solve equations, we find the value/ solution/ root of the unknown/ variable in the equation.

Example: When we solve x − 3 = 1 , we find x = 4 .

4. Equivalent equations are equations that give the same solution.

Example: x−3=1 and 5x−18=2 are equivalent equations as x=4 is the solution

to both equations.

5. A linear equation is an equation in which the highest power of the variable/unknown is 1.

Example: 5x −18 = 2 is a linear equation.
