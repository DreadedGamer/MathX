Terminology

1. Numbers with the sign “ – ” are called negative numbers. “ – ”is read as “negative”.

E.g. –1, –2, –3, .....

2. Integers (Z) are positive or negative whole numbers. E.g. ., –3, –2, –1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ....

3. Positive integers (Z+) are numbers that are greater than zero. E.g. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ....

4. Zero is an integer that is neither positive nor negative.

6. <, ≤, > and ≥ are inequality signs.

- “<” means “smaller than”,

- “>” means “greater than”,

- “ ” means “smaller than or equals to”,

- “ ” means “greater than or equals to”.

7. Rational numbers (Q) are numbers that can be expressed in the form ba , where a and b are integers and b ≠ 0 . E.g. All fractions and whole numbers.

8. Irrational numbers are numbers that are not rational. E.g. π, 2 , −3 4 . More on irrational numbers on next lesson.

 

9. Real Numbers (R) are numbers that can be found on the number line. They are made up of

rational and irrational numbers.

10. The numerical or absolute value of a number, x, written as |x| , is its distance from zero on the number line. The absolute value of an integer is always positive since distance is never negative.

E.g. | 1 | = 1, | -2 | = 2. | -5 | = 5

Rules for adding two integers

Case 1: To add two positive numbers, add their absolute values. The result is positive.

(+x)+(+y)=+(x+y)

Case 2: To add two negative numbers, add their absolute values and place a negative sign

before the result.

(−x)+(−y)=−(x+y)

Case 3: To add a positive and a negative number, find the difference of their absolute values by subtracting the smaller absolute value from the larger absolute value. Place the sign of the number having the larger absolute value before the result.

For any x > 0, y > 0,

(+x)+(−y)=+(x−y), ifx>y

(+x)+(−y)=−(y−x), ify>x

(−x)+(+y)=−(x−y), ifx>y

(−x)+(+y)=+(y−x), ify>x

                                

Note:

Definition: The reciprocal of a number x is 1/x.

To divide integers, find the reciprocal of the integer being divided (divisor) and multiply the first

 integer according to the rules for multiplication of integers.

You may think of dividing a number as an abbreviation of multiplying its reciprocal!

x ÷ y = x × 1/y
